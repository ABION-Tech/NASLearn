International Trade Agreements

1. Overview of Key Trade Agreements and Organizations

Explanation: International trade agreements and organizations help regulate and facilitate trade between countries by reducing barriers and providing a framework for trade relations.

Key Concepts:

  • World Trade Organization (WTO): An international body that sets global rules for trade and helps resolve trade disputes.
  • North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA) / USMCA: A free trade agreement between the U.S., Canada, and Mexico, now updated as the USMCA.
  • European Union (EU): A political and economic union of 27 European countries that allows for free trade and the movement of goods, services, capital, and people.
  • Bilateral and Multilateral Agreements: Trade agreements between two (bilateral) or more (multilateral) countries to reduce tariffs and other trade barriers.
