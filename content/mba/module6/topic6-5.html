Practice Exercises

1. Exercise 1: Conduct a SWOT Analysis

Select an organization of your choice (e.g., Starbucks, Apple, or a local business). Conduct a SWOT analysis of the organization, identifying key internal and external factors that influence its strategic decisions.

Solution Example (Apple):
Strengths: Strong brand, loyal customer base, innovative products.
Weaknesses: High product prices, reliance on iPhone sales.
Opportunities: Expansion into services (e.g., Apple TV+), growing demand for wearables.
Threats: Intense competition (Samsung, Google), regulatory challenges in international markets.

2. Exercise 2: Strategy Formulation

You are a strategist for a mid-sized tech company. Develop a business-level strategy to help your company compete against industry giants like Amazon and Google. Consider whether cost leadership, differentiation, or focus is the best approach.

Solution:
If the company is small and lacks the scale to compete on cost, a focus strategy with a differentiation approach in niche markets (e.g., specialized enterprise software solutions) may be more effective.

Assignment: Competitive Analysis

Assignment:

Conduct a competitive analysis of a company in the tech or retail sector. Evaluate its competitors, identify key differentiating factors, and provide strategic recommendations for gaining competitive advantage.

Key Components to Consider:

1. Identify the Company: Choose a specific company in the tech or retail sector for analysis.
2. Competitor Analysis: List main competitors and evaluate their market positions, strengths, and weaknesses.
3. Differentiating Factors: Identify what sets the chosen company apart from its competitors, such as unique products, customer service, or branding.
4. Strategic Recommendations: Based on the analysis, provide actionable recommendations for the company to enhance its competitive advantage.

Formatting Guidelines:

  • Length: 2-3 pages
  • Font: Times New Roman, 12pt
  • References: Include a bibliography with at least three sources.
