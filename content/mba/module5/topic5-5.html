1. Exercise 1:

A company currently holds $500,000 worth of inventory and generates annual sales of $5,000,000. Calculate the inventory turnover ratio and explain its significance.

Solution:

Inventory Turnover Ratio = Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory

Inventory Turnover Ratio = 5,000,000 / 500,000 = 10

This means the company turns over its inventory 10 times a year, indicating efficient inventory management.
