Practice Exercises

1. Exercise 1:

Given the following financial data for a hypothetical company, calculate key ratios:

  • Current Assets: $150,000
  • Current Liabilities: $100,000
  • Net Income: $50,000
  • Shareholders’ Equity: $250,000
  • Total Debt: $120,000

Solution:

  • Current Ratio: 150,000 / 100,000 = 1.5
  • Return on Equity (ROE): 50,000 / 250,000 = 20%
  • Debt-to-Equity Ratio: 120,000 / 250,000 = 0.48 (or 48%)

2. Exercise 2:

A company is evaluating two investment opportunities:

  • Project A: Requires an initial investment of $100,000 and generates cash flows of $30,000 annually for 5 years.
  • Project B: Requires an initial investment of $150,000 and generates cash flows of $40,000 annually for 5 years.

Calculate the NPV for both projects assuming a discount rate of 8%.

Solution:

Use the NPV formula:

NPV = ∑ Cash Flow / (1 + r)t - Initial Investment
