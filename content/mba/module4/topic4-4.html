Risk Management

Identifying and Managing Financial Risks

Explanation:

Financial risk management involves identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks to a companyâ€™s finances. Key financial risks include:

  • Market Risk: Exposure to changes in market prices (e.g., interest rates, foreign exchange rates).
  • Credit Risk: The risk of a counterparty defaulting on a debt.
  • Liquidity Risk: The risk of not being able to meet short-term obligations.

Real-World Example:

Airlines hedge against fuel price fluctuations by entering into futures contracts, reducing their exposure to rising fuel costs.

Significance:

Effective risk management ensures that companies can withstand financial shocks and continue operating in uncertain environments.
