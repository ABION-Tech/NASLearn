Investment Decisions

Time Value of Money (TVM)

Explanation:

The concept that money today is worth more than the same amount in the future due to its potential earning capacity. TVM is the foundation of investment decision-making.

  • Present Value (PV): The value of future cash flows discounted back to today.
  • Future Value (FV): The value of an investment after earning interest over time.

Key Concepts:

  • Discounting and Compounding: Central to determining present and future value.
  • Interest Rates: Play a critical role in calculating TVM.

Real-World Example:

Significance:

Understanding TVM ensures sound investment decisions and capital budgeting.

Capital Budgeting Techniques

Explanation:

Capital budgeting is the process of evaluating investment projects to determine their worth. Two key methods are:

  • Net Present Value (NPV): Calculates the present value of cash flows from an investment, subtracting the initial cost. A positive NPV indicates a good investment.
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR): The discount rate that makes the NPV of a project zero. If the IRR exceeds the required rate of return, the project is considered favorable.

Real-World Example:

A company deciding whether to build a new factory would use NPV and IRR to evaluate the long-term profitability of the project.

Significance:

These techniques help businesses make informed decisions about which projects to pursue, ensuring investments are aligned with financial goals.
