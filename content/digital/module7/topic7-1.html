Introduction to Google Analytics

Definition:

Google Analytics is a web analytics service that tracks and reports website traffic, providing insights into user behavior, acquisition, and engagement.

Key Features:

  • Real-Time Data: Monitor user activity as it happens on the website.
  • Audience Reports: Understand user demographics, interests, and behavior patterns.
  • Acquisition Reports: Analyze how users arrive at the site (organic search, paid search, social media, referrals).
  • Behavior Reports: Observe user interactions on the site, including page views, bounce rates, and time spent on pages.

Significance:

Google Analytics enables marketers to make informed decisions based on data, helping to refine marketing strategies and improve ROI.
