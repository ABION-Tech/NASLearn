1. Scope Definition Challenges

Question: What challenges might arise when defining project scope?

Key Points to Consider:

  • Unclear or conflicting project objectives.
  • Stakeholder disagreements on priorities.
  • Inadequate understanding of constraints and limitations.
  • Risk of scope creep due to poorly defined boundaries.

2. Stakeholder Engagement

Question: How can ineffective stakeholder management derail a project?

Key Points to Consider:

  • Misaligned expectations leading to dissatisfaction.
  • Lack of stakeholder input causing delays or rework.
  • Reduced buy-in or resistance from key parties.
  • Missed opportunities to address potential risks early.

3. Charter Utility

Question: How does a project charter set the tone for a project's success?

Key Points to Consider:

  • Establishes clear objectives and scope, minimizing ambiguity.
  • Aligns stakeholders with shared goals and expectations.
  • Provides a roadmap for decision-making and issue resolution.
  • Serves as a reference to maintain focus and accountability.

4. Real-World Applications

Question: Share an example of a project where strong initiation practices led to success.

Key Points to Consider:

  • Example: Launching a new product with a well-defined charter outlining market research, development milestones, and a go-to-market strategy.
  • Highlight how clear scope, stakeholder engagement, and initial planning contributed to on-time delivery, budget adherence, and market success.
