Project Management: Module 2 - Project Initiation

This module focuses on the foundational phase of project management, emphasizing the importance of clear objectives, stakeholder engagement, and formal documentation for successful project execution.

Topic 2: Stakeholder Analysis

Stakeholder analysis is a critical step in ensuring project success by identifying, categorizing, and engaging with key stakeholders effectively.

Who Are Stakeholders?

Stakeholders are individuals, groups, or organizations that are affected by or can influence the project.

  • Clients
  • Team Members
  • Suppliers
  • Regulatory Bodies

Steps to Stakeholder Analysis

The following steps provide a structured approach to stakeholder analysis:

  1. Identify All Stakeholders: Create a comprehensive list of people or organizations involved in or affected by the project.
  2. Categorize Stakeholders: Classify stakeholders based on their influence on and interest in the project.
  3. Develop Engagement Strategies: Tailor strategies for each category, such as keeping stakeholders informed, consulting them, or closely managing their involvement.

Practical Tools

Several tools can help streamline the stakeholder analysis process:

  • Stakeholder Matrix: A visual tool to map stakeholders based on their power and interest levels.

Example: For a marketing campaign:

  • Clients: High-interest and high-power stakeholders who require active engagement.
  • Public: High-interest but low-power stakeholders who need to be kept informed.

Practice Tasks

  1. Identify key stakeholders for a software development project and classify them using a stakeholder matrix.
  2. Create an engagement strategy for a regulatory body involved in a construction project.
  3. Develop a list of communication methods (e.g., email updates, weekly meetings) to keep stakeholders informed.
