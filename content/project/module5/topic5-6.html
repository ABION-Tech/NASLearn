Module 5: Risk Management

Discussion Questions & Assignments

Discussion Questions

Reflect on the concepts learned in this module and discuss the following questions with your peers:

  1. Risk Identification: What are the most common risks in project management? Provide examples from different industries.
  2. Proactive vs. Reactive Risk Management: Compare proactive and reactive risk management approaches. When is each approach more effective?
  3. Risk Prioritization: How do you determine which risks need immediate attention? Discuss different risk assessment techniques such as risk matrices.
  4. Risk Mitigation Strategies: Share examples of effective risk mitigation strategies you have encountered in real-life projects or case studies.
  5. Failure & Lessons Learned: Discuss a project that failed due to poor risk management. What lessons can be learned from it?

Assignment: Risk Management Plan Development

For this assignment, you will create a **Risk Management Plan** for a hypothetical or real project.

Task 1: Project Selection

Choose a project (either a real-life project or a hypothetical one) in one of the following industries:

  • Technology (e.g., software development, AI implementation)
  • Construction (e.g., building a residential complex)
  • Healthcare (e.g., launching a new medical device)
  • Finance (e.g., introducing a new banking service)

Task 2: Risk Identification

Identify at least **five** potential risks associated with your selected project.

Task 3: Risk Assessment

For each identified risk, assess the **likelihood** (Low, Medium, High) and **impact** (Minor, Moderate, Critical) using a risk matrix.

Task 4: Risk Mitigation Strategies

Develop a mitigation plan for each risk, detailing:

  • Preventive actions to reduce risk probability.
  • Contingency plans in case the risk occurs.
  • Key stakeholders responsible for risk management.

Task 5: Risk Monitoring Plan

Outline how you will monitor risks throughout the project lifecycle. Include:

  • Review frequency (weekly, monthly, or milestone-based).
  • Reporting structure (who reports to whom).
  • Response triggers (at what point action should be taken).

Submission Format

Submit a structured document (PDF or Word) with the following sections:

  1. Project Overview
  2. Identified Risks
  3. Risk Assessment Table
  4. Mitigation Strategies
  5. Monitoring Plan
  6. Conclusion & Key Takeaways

Bonus Challenge: Risk Response Simulation

In groups, simulate a project risk scenario where an unexpected issue occurs. One team acts as the project team, while the other plays the role of stakeholders. How do you manage the situation?
