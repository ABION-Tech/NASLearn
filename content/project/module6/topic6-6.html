Hands-On Practice: Building Your First Automated Welcome Series

Objective

To apply the concepts of email marketing automation by building a fundamental nurture workflow, a 3-step welcome series for new subscribers. This practice will solidify your understanding of behavioral triggers, email design, content sequencing, and list management.

Why This Matters

The welcome series is one of the highest-performing automated workflows you can create. It sets the tone for your relationship with a new subscriber, delivers immediate value, and begins guiding them toward their first conversion.

Scenario: The "Urban Jungle" Plant Shop

You are the digital marketer for "Urban Jungle," a hypothetical online store selling unique houseplants and accessories. A visitor to the website signs up for the newsletter to receive a lead magnet: "The Beginner's Guide to Not Killing Your Plants."

Your task is to welcome this new subscriber and encourage them to make their first purchase.

Your Challenge: Build the Automation

Step 1: Plan Your Series (The Blueprint)

Before you even open an email tool, you must plan your strategy. Answer these questions to build your blueprint:

  • Goal: What is the primary goal of this welcome series? (e.g., Build trust? Deliver the guide? Encourage a first purchase?)
  • Audience: Who are you emailing? What is their primary need or fear when they sign up?
  • Trigger: What specific user action will start this automated series?
Email 1 (Immediate)
  • Objective: What must this email accomplish immediately after signup?
  • Content: What is the core message? What is the Call-to-Action (CTA)?
Email 2 (24 Hours Later)
  • Objective: What is the logical next step in building this relationship?
  • Content: How will you provide further value? How will you introduce your products without being overly salesy? What is the CTA?
Email 3 (72 Hours Later)
  • Objective: What is the final nudge toward your primary goal?
  • Content: How will you incentivize action? What is the final CTA?
Step 2: Build the Workflow in a Tool

Now, implement your plan. You may use any email marketing platform you have access to (e.g., Mailchimp, Brevo (formerly Sendinblue), HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, many offer free tiers).

A. Find the Automation Builder

Locate the section in your chosen platform for creating automated email sequences, often called "Automation," "Workflows," or "Journeys."

B. Set the Trigger

Configure the workflow to start when a contact is added to a specific list or when a form is submitted.

C. Add the Emails

Build the three emails you planned in Step 1.

  • Craft compelling subject lines that encourage opens.
  • Design the email body using the principles of hierarchy, scannability, and mobile optimization.
  • Include clear, prominent CTAs in each email.
  • Use personalization tags (like {{First Name}}) where appropriate.
  • Set the delays between emails (e.g., send Email 2 after 24 hours, Email 3 after 72 hours).
Step 3: Test and Validate

A crucial, and often skipped, step. Never launch an automation without testing it first.

  • Enroll a Test Email: Add your own personal email address to the list that triggers the automation.
  • Experience the Journey: Go through the entire series as a subscriber would. Open every email. Click every link.
  • Check for Errors:
    • Are all the links working correctly?
    • Is the personalization populating correctly?
    • Do the emails look good on your mobile phone?
    • Did the emails arrive at the correct time intervals?
  • Refine: Make any necessary adjustments based on your test.
