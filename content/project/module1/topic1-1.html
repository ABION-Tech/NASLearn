Module 1: Introduction to Project Management

1. Basics of Project Management

Definition and Importance of Project Management:

Project management involves the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements. It is crucial for ensuring that a project is completed successfully, on time, within budget, and with the desired outcomes.

Key Principles of Effective Project Management:

  • Clear Objectives: Establishing a clear vision and specific goals for the project.
  • Planning: Developing a detailed plan that outlines the tasks, resources, and schedule required.
  • Risk Management: Identifying and mitigating potential risks throughout the project lifecycle.
  • Communication: Ensuring open and regular communication between team members and stakeholders.
  • Monitoring and Control: Tracking progress and making adjustments as necessary to stay on course.

Differences Between Project Management and General Management:

  • Scope: Project management focuses on specific, temporary goals, while general management oversees the ongoing operation of an organization.
  • Duration: Project management is typically short-term with a defined end, while general management is continuous and ongoing.
  • Focus: Project management is focused on delivering a specific product or outcome, whereas general management involves overall business operations and long-term strategic goals.
