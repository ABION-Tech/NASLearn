Module 1: Introduction to Project Management

2. Key Terminologies in Project Management

Overview of Common Terms:

  • Scope: Defines the boundaries of the project, including what is included and excluded from the project.
  • Milestones: Key events or stages in the project timeline, used to measure progress.
  • Deliverables: Specific outputs or outcomes that the project must produce upon completion.
  • Stakeholders: Individuals or groups who have an interest in the project’s outcome, including team members, customers, and sponsors.
  • Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): A hierarchical decomposition of the project scope into manageable sections.
  • Risk: Any potential event or condition that may affect the project negatively.

Introduction to Project Management Methodologies:

Project management methodologies are approaches used to plan, execute, and manage projects. The choice of methodology depends on the project’s needs and environment. Some common methodologies include:

  • Agile: An iterative approach that focuses on flexibility, collaboration, and delivering small, incremental changes.
  • Waterfall: A linear and sequential approach where each phase of the project must be completed before moving to the next.
  • Scrum: A subset of Agile, Scrum emphasizes teamwork, accountability, and iterative progress with defined roles like Scrum Master and Product Owner.
  • Kanban: Another Agile approach, focusing on visualizing work and improving flow by managing work in progress.

Understanding Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for Project Success:

  • Time: Measures whether the project is completed on schedule.
  • Cost: Evaluates whether the project stays within the approved budget.
  • Quality: Assesses if the project deliverables meet the required standards and expectations.
  • Scope: Tracks whether the project’s scope has changed or expanded beyond the original plan.
  • Stakeholder Satisfaction: Measures the satisfaction of stakeholders, including customers and project sponsors.
  • Resource Utilization: Looks at how efficiently resources (human, financial, and material) are being used in the project.
