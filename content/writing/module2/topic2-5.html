Assignment: Sentence Rewriting

Objective: Transform poorly written sentences into clear and grammatically correct ones.

Instructions:

1. Rewrite the following sentences:

  • Original: *The report was written by John and is being reviewed by Sarah.*
    Error: Overuse of passive voice.
    Rewrite: John wrote the report, and Sarah is reviewing it.
    Explanation: Rewriting in active voice makes the sentence clearer and more concise. Active voice gives a direct, straightforward approach to communication.
  • Original: *During the meeting, we discussed project updates, however, we didn’t make any decisions.*
    Error: Incorrect use of a comma instead of a semicolon before "however."
    Rewrite: During the meeting, we discussed project updates; however, we didn’t make any decisions.
    Explanation: A semicolon is necessary to separate two independent clauses when using "however." This helps avoid comma splices and ensures proper sentence structure.
  • Original: *He don’t have no idea about the new policies.*
    Error: Double negative and incorrect verb form.
    Rewrite: He doesn’t have any idea about the new policies.
    Explanation: Corrected the double negative ("don’t" and "no") to avoid confusion and used the appropriate conjugation of "do" for the third-person singular subject ("doesn't").
  • Original: *The cake baked by my sister, it was delicious.*
    Error: Sentence fragment due to a misplaced clause.
    Rewrite: The cake baked by my sister was delicious.
    Explanation: Removing the comma and connecting the subject with the predicate makes the sentence grammatically complete. The sentence now properly expresses a single, coherent thought.
  • Original: *I have went to the store already.*
    Error: Incorrect verb form ("went" instead of "gone").
    Rewrite: I have gone to the store already.
    Explanation: "Gone" is the correct past participle form of "go" used with the present perfect tense ("have gone"), whereas "went" is the simple past tense, which doesn’t work with "have."

2. For each correction:

  • Highlight the grammar error.
  • Explain why the change improves the sentence.

Submission Requirements:

- Submit the rewritten sentences and explanations as a Word document or PDF.

Evaluation Criteria:

  • Grammar accuracy.
  • Sentence clarity.
  • Thoroughness of explanations.
