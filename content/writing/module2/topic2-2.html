Topic 2: Common Grammar Mistakes

Sentence Fragments

A sentence fragment is an incomplete sentence that lacks either a subject or predicate (or both). It does not express a complete thought.

Example: Went to the park.

Corrected: She went to the park.

Run-On Sentences

A run-on sentence occurs when two independent clauses (complete thoughts) are improperly joined together without proper punctuation or conjunctions.

Example: He loves reading he bought many books.

Corrected: He loves reading, so he bought many books.

Misplaced Modifiers

Misplaced modifiers are words or phrases that are placed awkwardly in a sentence, leading to confusion or ambiguity about what is being described.

Example: She saw a bird sitting on the bench.

Corrected: While sitting on the bench, she saw a bird.

These common mistakes can impact the clarity and meaning of your writing. Recognizing and correcting them helps ensure more precise and effective communication.
