Module 2: Grammar and Sentence Structure

Topic 1: Sentence Basics

What Is a Sentence?

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought. It typically consists of a subject, predicate, and, optionally, objects or modifiers.

Components of a Sentence:

  • Subject - The person, place, thing, or idea performing the action or being described.
    Example: The cat sleeps.
  • Predicate - Describes the action or state of the subject.
    Example: The cat sleeps.
  • Objects - Receive the action of the verb.
    Example: She reads a book.

Examples of Sentence Types:

1. Simple Sentence

Contains a subject and a predicate.

Example: The cat sleeps.

2. Compound Sentence

Combines two independent clauses with a coordinating conjunction (e.g., and, but, or).

Example: The cat sleeps, and the dog barks.

3. Complex Sentence

Combines an independent clause with one or more dependent clauses.

Example: Although the cat sleeps, the dog barks.
