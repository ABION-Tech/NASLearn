Topic 3: Improving Clarity

Active vs. Passive Voice

Active Voice: The subject of the sentence performs the action.

Example: The manager approved the proposal.

Passive Voice: The subject of the sentence receives the action.

Example: The proposal was approved by the manager.

When to Use Active vs. Passive Voice

Active Voice:

  • Ideal for clear and direct communication.
  • Suitable for most situations, especially when the actor (subject) is important.

Example: She completed the project on time.

Passive Voice:

  • Useful for emphasizing the action itself or when the actor is unknown or irrelevant.

Example: The project was completed on time.

Tips for Clarity

  • Avoid including too many ideas in a single sentence, as it can confuse readers.

    • Example: Although the weather was bad and the roads were slippery, we decided to drive to the city because the meeting was important.

    Simplified: Despite the bad weather and slippery roads, we drove to the city for the important meeting.

  • Use precise words and steer clear of jargon or overly complex phrases.

    • Example: Instead of utilize resources effectively, say use resources effectively.

Prioritizing clarity in writing ensures your message is easy to understand and impactful.
