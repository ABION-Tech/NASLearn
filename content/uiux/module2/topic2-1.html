UI/UX: Module 2: User Research and Personas

This module delves into how user research informs design decisions and the process of creating user personas to represent target audiences.

Topic 1: User Research Techniques

Why Conduct User Research?

Understand user needs, behaviors, and pain points to design more effective and user-centered solutions.

Methods:

  • Interviews: Direct, open-ended conversations with users to gather qualitative insights into their experiences and expectations.
  • Surveys: Structured questionnaires designed to collect quantitative data on user preferences and behaviors.
  • Observation: Watching users interact with a product or service in real-time to uncover usability issues or workflow challenges.

Tools for User Research:

  • Google Forms, Typeform: Tools for creating and distributing surveys easily.
  • Note-taking Apps: Tools like Evernote or Notion for documenting user interviews and observations effectively.
