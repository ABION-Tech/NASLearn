UI/UX: Module 2 - User Research and Personas

This module explores how user research informs design decisions and the process of creating user personas to represent target audiences. Understanding your audience and using insights from research ensures that designs meet user needs effectively.

Topic 3: Using Insights for Design

Insights from user research are invaluable for creating user-centered designs. By understanding your target audience, you can design experiences that are intuitive, efficient, and impactful.

Why Insights Matter

Using insights effectively benefits both the design team and the end user:

  • Aligns Design Goals with User Needs: Insights ensure that the design focuses on solving real problems users face.
  • Reduces Design Revisions: A user-centered approach minimizes the need for costly and time-consuming iterations.

How to Use Personas

Personas are fictional yet data-driven representations of target users. They provide a framework for making informed design decisions:

  • Prioritize Features Based on Persona Needs: Focus on functionality that aligns with the preferences and pain points of your personas.
  • Test Designs with Personas in Mind: Evaluate prototypes by considering how well they address the persona’s goals and challenges.

Practical Example

Scenario: Sarah is a persona representing eco-conscious users who value simplicity. She often shops online but dislikes lengthy checkout processes. Based on these insights:

  1. Design Goal: Create a streamlined checkout process that minimizes clicks and distractions.
  2. Implementation:
    • Use autofill options for returning users.
    • Offer a one-page checkout with clearly labeled fields.
    • Highlight eco-friendly shipping options prominently.
