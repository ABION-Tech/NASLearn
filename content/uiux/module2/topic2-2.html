UI/UX: Module 2: User Research and Personas

This module delves into how user research informs design decisions and the process of creating user personas to represent target audiences.

Topic 2: Creating Personas

What Are Personas?

Personas are fictional characters created from user research to represent distinct segments of the target audience. These personas help teams empathize with users and make design decisions that are better aligned with real user needs. By using personas, designers can better understand the challenges, goals, and behaviors of the people they are designing for, leading to more user-centered and successful product designs.

Components of a Persona:

  • Name and Photo: Humanizing the persona with a relatable identity allows design teams to emotionally connect with the user they represent. Including a name, a photo, or avatar makes it easier to imagine real users rather than abstract concepts.
  • Demographics: These provide context about the persona, such as age, gender, location, occupation, and education level. This information helps create realistic and representative profiles that guide design decisions.
  • Goals and Challenges: A persona's goals define what they want to achieve using the product, while their challenges illustrate the barriers they face in achieving those goals. Identifying these helps ensure that the product design addresses user needs effectively.
  • Behavioral Patterns and Preferences: These include daily routines, preferences, frustrations, and the tools the persona uses. Understanding the persona’s behavior ensures that the design fits into their lifestyle and addresses their preferences, improving overall usability.

Example Persona:

Name: Sarah

Age: 34

Location: New York

Goals:
  • Discover eco-friendly products online that align with her values.
  • Simplify her shopping experience, ensuring it’s quick and intuitive.
Challenges:
  • Struggles with navigating complex e-commerce platforms that offer an overwhelming variety of options.
  • Finds it hard to differentiate between genuinely sustainable products and those that are merely marketed as such.

This persona represents a user segment that values sustainability and is tech-savvy but feels frustrated with the lack of transparency in the e-commerce space. Understanding Sarah’s goals and challenges helps in designing a more user-friendly, intuitive platform that makes it easy for her to find sustainable products and navigate the shopping process.
