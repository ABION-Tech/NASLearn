About This Course

Welcome to the User Interface and User Experience Design Course at the New Age Skillsphere (NAS). This course is designed to provide you with the essential skills needed to design intuitive, user-friendly interfaces and create seamless user experiences for digital products. Whether you are new to design or looking to enhance your design thinking and practical skills, this course will provide you with a comprehensive foundation in UI/UX design principles, tools, and techniques.

Throughout the course, you will engage with expert-led lectures, hands-on projects, case studies, and collaborative activities. You will learn how to design effective user interfaces and enhance user experiences using industry-standard tools such as Figma, Sketch, Adobe XD, and others. You'll explore key concepts like wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, user research, and interaction design. You'll also dive into the principles of visual design, responsive design, and accessibility to ensure your designs meet user needs and business goals.

Our unique learning model ensures you gain both theoretical knowledge and practical experience in UI/UX design. You will develop the ability to create aesthetically pleasing and functional interfaces, conduct user-centered research, and iterate on designs based on feedback. The course also includes an internship, where you'll work on real-world design projects, allowing you to apply your skills in a professional setting and preparing you for success in the fast-evolving field of UI/UX design.

Course Curriculum

Module 1: Introduction to UI/UX Design

Understand the basics of UI/UX design, its importance, and its role in creating user-centered products. Learn key terminology and concepts.

Module 2: User Research and Personas

Learn how to conduct user research, create user personas, and use insights to inform design decisions and create better user experiences.

Module 3: Wireframing and Prototyping

Explore the process of creating wireframes and prototypes to test ideas quickly and iteratively refine designs.

Module 4: Visual Design Principles

Understand color theory, typography, layout, and other visual design principles to create visually appealing and functional interfaces.

Module 5: Interaction Design

Learn how to design interactive elements, including buttons, forms, and animations, that enhance user engagement and usability.

Module 6: Design Tools and Software

Get hands-on experience with industry-standard tools like Figma, Adobe XD, Sketch, and others for UI/UX design and collaboration.

Module 7: Accessibility and Inclusive Design

Learn how to create accessible designs that are inclusive of all users, including those with disabilities, following WCAG guidelines.

Module 8: Usability Testing

Conduct usability testing to gather user feedback, identify pain points, and refine designs for better user experiences.

Module 9: Design Systems and Guidelines

Understand the role of design systems in maintaining consistency and scalability in design projects across teams.

Week 10: Capstone Project Execution

Prepare for your internship by focusing on industry expectations and professional development.

Week 11 - 12: Elective Classes

Student shall embark on a two-week journey on any 2 electives availble on our catalogue to broaden their horizons.

Week 13: Internship Preparation

Prepare for your internship by focusing on industry expectations and professional development.

Week 14-23: Internship Proper

Student shall focus on internship aiming to gain industry experience and professional development.

Week 24: Final Completion and Graduation

Student shall finalize all thier works and recieve a certificate on Graduation day, for their hard work in the class session and their dedication during the internship.
