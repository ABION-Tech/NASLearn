Week 10: Capstone Project - Project Planning

1. Individual or Group Meetings for Guidance

As you work on your capstone project, it is essential to have regular meetings with your instructor or classmates to get guidance and feedback. These meetings can help you stay on track, identify potential challenges, and ensure that your project is progressing smoothly. Consider scheduling:

  • Weekly check-ins to discuss progress and obstacles.
  • Bi-weekly presentations to showcase findings and receive critiques.
  • Peer review sessions to foster collaboration and support.

2. Project Proposal

Your project proposal should provide a clear overview of your project, including:

  • Problem Statement: A concise and well-defined statement of the problem you are trying to solve.
  • Goals and Objectives: Specific and measurable goals for your project, outlining what success looks like.
  • Dataset: A description of the dataset you will be using, including its size, format, and relevant features. Include details such as:
    • Source of the dataset (public or proprietary).
    • Expected challenges in data acquisition or manipulation.
  • Methodology: A brief outline of the machine learning techniques you plan to use, such as:
    • Supervised vs. Unsupervised learning.
    • Specific algorithms (e.g., regression, classification).
    • Tools and libraries you plan to utilize (e.g., TensorFlow, Scikit-learn).
  • Timeline: A schedule for completing the project, including key milestones and deadlines. Consider using:
    • Gantt charts to visualize project timelines.
    • Tools like Trello or Asana for task management.
  • Evaluation Metrics: The metrics you will use to evaluate the performance of your model, such as:
    • Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-Score.
    • ROC-AUC curves for classification problems.
  • Potential Challenges and Solutions: A discussion of potential challenges you may face during the project and how you plan to address them, including:
    • Data quality issues.
    • Time management challenges.

Assignment: Submit a Capstone Project Proposal

Submit your project proposal to your instructor for review and feedback. Make sure your proposal is well-structured, clear, and concise. Aim for:

  • Professional formatting and presentation.
  • Clear articulation of your ideas and strategies.
  • Inclusion of relevant citations and references.

Additional Tips for Success

  • **Start Early:** Give yourself plenty of time to complete your project and avoid last-minute stress.
  • **Seek Feedback:** Get feedback from your instructor and classmates throughout the project to refine your approach.
  • **Be Flexible:** Be prepared to adjust your plans as needed based on feedback and findings.
  • **Document Your Work:** Keep a detailed record of your progress and decisions to facilitate reflection and future projects.
