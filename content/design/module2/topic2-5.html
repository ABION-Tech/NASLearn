Graphic Design: Module 2: Assignment: Flyer Redesign Challenge

Objective:

Redesign an existing flyer or poster by incorporating design principles to improve clarity and visual appeal.

Steps:

  1. Select a Flyer or Poster:

    Choose a simple design, such as a community event flyer, local business poster, or promotional ad.

  2. Analyze the Current Design:

    Review the flyer for issues in:

    • Balance: Is the design visually stable?
    • Contrast: Are elements distinct enough to highlight key information?
    • Alignment: Are elements organized and cleanly arranged?
    • Hierarchy: Is the most important information prioritized and easy to locate?

  3. Redesign:

    Utilize tools like Canva, Adobe Photoshop, or Illustrator.

    Focus on applying principles:

    • Balance: Distribute visual weight evenly.
    • Contrast: Use colors, fonts, or sizes to create focal points.
    • Proximity: Group related information for better readability.
    • Alignment: Arrange elements neatly for a polished appearance.

    Ensure a clear visual hierarchy to guide viewers' attention.

  4. Submit:

    Include both the original and redesigned versions of the flyer or poster.

    Write a 200â€“300 word explanation detailing:

    • What issues were identified in the original design.
    • How the redesign addressed these issues.
    • Which design principles were applied and how they improved the overall composition.

Evaluation Criteria:

  • Effective Use of Design Principles: Clearly demonstrates improvements in balance, contrast, proximity, alignment, and hierarchy.
  • Improved Clarity and Aesthetic Appeal: The redesign should make the flyer more visually engaging and easier to read.
  • Creativity and Attention to Detail: Showcase innovation while maintaining professionalism in the design.

This challenge enables students to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world design tasks, developing both analytical and creative skills.
