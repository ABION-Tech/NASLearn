Types of Chatbots and Their Applications

1. Rule-Based Chatbots

Rule-based chatbots operate using predefined rules and decision trees. They follow a structured flow to respond to user queries.

Characteristics:

  • Uses a fixed set of responses.
  • Cannot handle complex queries beyond pre-programmed paths.
  • Often used for FAQs and basic customer support.

Examples:

  • Automated banking assistants for balance inquiries.
  • Support bots answering common product-related questions.

2. AI-Powered Chatbots

AI-powered chatbots use machine learning (ML) and natural language processing (NLP) to understand and respond to user input dynamically.

Characteristics:

  • Continuously learns from interactions.
  • Can handle complex conversations with contextual understanding.
  • Requires data training to improve performance.

Examples:

  • AI customer service bots (e.g., chatbots used by telecom companies).
  • Healthcare bots for medical consultations.

3. Hybrid Chatbots

Hybrid chatbots combine rule-based structures with AI capabilities, allowing them to follow predefined paths while also learning from data.

Characteristics:

  • Provides structured responses but adapts with AI.
  • Balances efficiency and complexity.
  • Useful in customer support and sales assistance.

Examples:

  • Retail customer support bots that escalate complex queries to human agents.
  • Travel booking bots that guide users through reservations.

4. Voice Assistants

Voice assistants are AI-driven chatbots that process voice commands and interact through speech.

Characteristics:

  • Uses speech recognition and NLP.
  • Can perform tasks such as setting reminders, controlling smart devices, and answering queries.
