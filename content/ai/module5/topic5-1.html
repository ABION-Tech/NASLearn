Week 5: Chatbots and Conversational AI

Introduction to Chatbots and Conversational AI

What Are Chatbots?

Chatbots are AI-powered software programs designed to simulate human-like conversations with users. They can be text-based or voice-based and are commonly used in customer service, marketing, and personal assistance applications.

What Is Conversational AI?

Conversational AI refers to the broader field of artificial intelligence that enables machines to understand, process, and respond to human language naturally. This includes chatbots, virtual assistants, and AI-powered customer service tools.

Key Components of a Chatbot

  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Allows chatbots to interpret and respond to user input.
  • Machine Learning (ML): Enables chatbots to learn from interactions and improve over time.
  • Knowledge Base: A database of predefined responses and information used by chatbots.
  • Integration with APIs: Allows chatbots to connect with external services like CRM or databases.

Why Are Chatbots Important?

Businesses and individuals use chatbots to automate tasks, enhance customer engagement, and provide quick responses without requiring human intervention. They improve efficiency and can operate 24/7, making them highly valuable in various industries.

Real-World Applications of Chatbots

  • Customer service automation (e.g., answering FAQs, handling complaints)
  • Personal assistants (e.g., Siri, Google Assistant, Alexa)
  • Healthcare (e.g., appointment scheduling, symptom checking)
  • E-commerce (e.g., guiding users through purchases, recommending products)
  • Education (e.g., tutoring, answering student queries)
