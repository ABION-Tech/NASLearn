Model Evaluation Metrics: R-squared and Mean Squared Error (MSE)

Evaluating the performance of a regression model is crucial to ensure its accuracy and reliability. R-squared and Mean Squared Error (MSE) are two commonly used metrics for assessing the quality of a regression model.

1. R-squared (R²)

Definition: R-squared measures the proportion of variance in the dependent variable that is explained by the independent variables.

Interpretation:

  • Values range from 0 to 1.
  • A higher R-squared value indicates that a larger proportion of the variance is explained by the model.
  • However, a high R-squared does not necessarily mean a good model if the data is not well-fitting or there is overfitting.

Calculation:

R² = 1 - (SSR / SST)

where:

  • SSR (Sum of Squared Residuals) is the sum of the squared differences between the predicted and actual values.
  • SST (Total Sum of Squares) is the sum of the squared differences between the actual values and the mean of the dependent variable.

2. Mean Squared Error (MSE)

Definition: MSE measures the average squared difference between the predicted and actual values.

Interpretation:

  • A lower MSE indicates a better-fitting model.
  • It is sensitive to outliers, as large errors are squared.

Calculation:

MSE = (1/n) * Σ(yᵢ - ŷᵢ)²

where:

  • n is the number of data points.
  • yᵢ is the actual value.
  • ŷᵢ is the predicted value.

Choosing the Right Metric

R-squared: Suitable for comparing models with different numbers of independent variables. However, it can be misleading if the data is not well-fitting or there is overfitting.

MSE: Sensitive to outliers and provides a direct measure of the average error.

Additional Considerations

  • Adjusted R-squared: Penalizes the addition of unnecessary independent variables, providing a more accurate measure when comparing models with different numbers of predictors.
  • Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE): The square root of MSE, providing a more interpretable measure in the same units as the dependent variable.
  • Relative Absolute Error (RAE): Measures the relative error compared to the mean of the dependent variable, giving context to the model's performance.
  • Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE): Measures the average percentage error between the predicted and actual values, allowing for a percentage-based interpretation of the model's accuracy.

By understanding and using these evaluation metrics, you can effectively assess the performance of your regression models and make informed decisions about model selection and tuning.
