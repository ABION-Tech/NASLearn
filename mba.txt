Here’s an updated and expanded curriculum for a 10-week MBA course, culminating in a capstone project in week 10. The course will cover essential MBA topics, integrating theoretical knowledge with practical applications to ensure a comprehensive learning experience.

### **10-Week MBA Course Curriculum**

---

#### **Week 1: Introduction to Business Administration**
- **Course Overview**
  - Objectives, expectations, and assessment methods.
- **Understanding Business Environments**
  - The role of businesses in the economy.
  - Types of business entities and structures.
- **Key Concepts in Business**
  - Overview of finance, marketing, operations, and management.
- **Assignments:**
  - Read introductory materials; write a reflective essay on the importance of business in society.

---

#### **Week 2: Leadership and Organizational Behavior**
- **Leadership Theories**
  - Transformational, transactional, and servant leadership.
  - Emotional intelligence in leadership.
- **Team Dynamics and Development**
  - Stages of team development and group dynamics.
  - Conflict resolution and negotiation skills.
- **Organizational Culture**
  - Definition, types, and impacts on performance.
  - Strategies for cultural change.
- **Assignments:**
  - Case study analysis on a successful leader; prepare a leadership style assessment.

---

#### **Week 3: Marketing Management**
- **Marketing Fundamentals**
  - The marketing mix: Product, Price, Place, Promotion.
- **Market Research Techniques**
  - Qualitative vs. quantitative research methods.
  - Analyzing consumer behavior and segmentation.
- **Branding and Positioning**
  - Developing a brand strategy.
  - Case studies of successful brands.
- **Assignments:**
  - Create a marketing plan for a hypothetical product.

---

#### **Week 4: Financial Management**
- **Financial Statements Overview**
  - Understanding the balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement.
- **Financial Analysis Tools**
  - Ratio analysis, trend analysis, and forecasting.
- **Investment Decisions**
  - Time value of money, capital budgeting techniques (NPV, IRR).
- **Risk Management**
  - Identifying and managing financial risks.
- **Assignments:**
  - Analyze financial statements of a company and present findings.

---

#### **Week 5: Operations and Supply Chain Management**
- **Operations Management Concepts**
  - Process design and improvement.
  - Quality management and continuous improvement methodologies (e.g., Six Sigma).
- **Supply Chain Fundamentals**
  - Components of supply chain management.
  - Inventory management techniques.
- **Technology in Operations**
  - Role of information systems in operations.
- **Assignments:**
  - Develop a process improvement plan for a chosen organization.

---

#### **Week 6: Strategic Management**
- **Strategic Planning Process**
  - Vision, mission, and values development.
  - SWOT analysis and competitive analysis.
- **Strategy Formulation and Implementation**
  - Business-level vs. corporate-level strategies.
  - Balanced scorecard approach.
- **Case Studies of Successful Strategies**
  - Analyze successful companies' strategic decisions.
- **Assignments:**
  - Conduct a SWOT analysis of a selected organization.

---

#### **Week 7: Business Ethics and Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)**
- **Understanding Business Ethics**
  - Ethical decision-making frameworks.
  - Common ethical dilemmas in business.
- **Corporate Governance**
  - Roles and responsibilities of the board of directors.
  - Regulatory compliance and ethical standards.
- **CSR Practices**
  - Importance of CSR in modern business.
  - Developing a CSR strategy.
- **Assignments:**
  - Write a paper on a recent ethical issue in business.

---

#### **Week 8: Entrepreneurial Thinking and Innovation**
- **Entrepreneurship Fundamentals**
  - Characteristics of successful entrepreneurs.
  - The entrepreneurial process and business models.
- **Innovation Management**
  - Types of innovation and how to foster a culture of innovation.
- **Venture Capital and Funding**
  - Sources of funding for startups.
  - Pitching to investors.
- **Assignments:**
  - Develop a business model canvas for a startup idea.

---

#### **Week 9: International Business and Global Strategy**
- **Global Business Environment**
  - Understanding global markets and trade theories.
  - Cultural considerations in international business.
- **Global Strategy Development**
  - Market entry strategies (exporting, franchising, joint ventures).
- **International Trade Agreements**
  - Overview of key trade agreements and organizations (e.g., WTO, NAFTA).
- **Assignments:**
  - Research and present a global market entry strategy for a local business.

---

#### **Week 10: Capstone Project**
- **Capstone Project Overview**
  - Introduction to the capstone project: Identifying a real-world business problem or opportunity.
  - Form teams or work individually based on interest.
- **Project Development**
  - Research, analyze, and develop solutions for the chosen problem.
  - Prepare a comprehensive report and presentation.
- **Final Presentation and Evaluation**
  - Present the project to faculty and industry experts.
  - Receive feedback and assess learning outcomes.
  
---

### **Assessment and Evaluation**
- **Participation and Engagement:** Weekly discussions and interactions.
- **Assignments:** Various assignments that reinforce learning and apply concepts.
- **Capstone Project:** Final project presentation and report evaluated on originality, research depth, and feasibility of solutions.

### **Learning Outcomes**
By the end of this course, students should be able to:
- Understand and apply core business concepts.
- Analyze financial statements and make informed financial decisions.
- Develop marketing strategies based on consumer behavior.
- Implement operations and supply chain strategies effectively.
- Create a comprehensive strategic plan for a business.
- Address ethical issues and understand the importance of CSR.
- Innovate and think entrepreneurially.
- Analyze global markets and develop international business strategies.

This comprehensive curriculum aims to provide a well-rounded MBA education, equipping students with the necessary skills to thrive in various business environments.
